The Fine Art of Talking Absolute Nonsense

While some people spend their time mastering chess or climbing mountains, I have dedicated my life to the most underrated skill in human history: talking absolute fluff.

Why use five words when you can use fifty? Why make a point when you can walk in verbal circles until everyone forgets why we started talking in the first place? To me, speaking is not about communication; it is about the rhythmic vibration of air molecules that carry zero functional data. It is a linguistic dance of pure, unadulterated gibberish.

In this paragraph, I am technically saying nothing, yet the words are arranged in a way that looks like a coherent thought. This is the peak of my hobby. It is the ability to sustain a conversation for thirty minutes without ever arriving at a conclusion. It is a masterpiece of vocal decoration.
